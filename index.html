The Life of Martin Sennet Conner

Mike Conner was an accomplished politician, lawyer, and statesman. He attended the University of Mississippi at age 14 (likely the youngest in history), where he was the two-time president of his fraternity (the only in the chapter's history). After graduating, he continued his education at Yale University, where he obtained his J.D. He returned to his home state and served as governor from 1932 — 1936.

Conner's term as governor was short-lived, but historic. During the Great Depression, he was able move the state of Mississippi into a budget surplus. Other noted accomplishments were his relationship with Louisiana's governor of the time, (Huey Pierce Long, Jr.), and their building of interstate roads, and his grace toward prisoners.

The former governor capped off his career as the first commissioner for the Southeastern Conference (that's right — the SEC, of Alabama and LSU fame). He died on September 16th, 1950 at the age of 59 and is buried in Jackson, Mississippi.
